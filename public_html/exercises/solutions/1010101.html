The usual metric on $\mathbb{R}$ is $d(x, y) = |x - y|$, for all $x, y \in \mathbb{R}$. We must check that this function $d$ is, in fact, a distance function on $\mathbb{R}$:
  • If $x = y$, then $|x - y| = 0$. If $d(x, y) = 0$, then $x - y = \pm 0 = 0$, so that $x = y$.
  • We have

    $d(x, y) = |x - y| = |-1(y - x)| = |y - x| = d(y, x)$

  • For all $z \in \mathbb{R}$,

    $\begin{array}{rcl} d(x, y) & = & |x - y| \\ & = & |x - z + z - y| \\ & \leq & |x - z| + |z - y| \\ & = & d(x, z) + d(z, y) \end{array}$
Since the axioms of a distance function are satisfied by $d$, $(\mathbb{R}, d)$ is a metric space.
