We must check whether $d(x, y) = \int_a^b |x(t) - y(t)| \, dt$ satisfies the three conditions of a distance function:
  • If $x = y$, then $x(t) - y(t) = 0$, for all $t \in [a, b]$, then $\int_a^b |x(t) - y(t)| \, dt = 0$. If $\int_a^b |x(t) - y(t)| \, dt = 0$, since the integrand is nonnegative, the integrand must be identically zero. Thus, $x(t) = y(t)$, for all $t \in [a, b]$; that is, $x = y$.
  • Since $|x(t) - y(t)| = |y(t) - x(t)|$ for each $t \in [a, b]$, we have

    $d(x, y) = \int_a^b |x(t) - y(t)| \, dt = \int_a^b |y(t) - x(t)| \, dt = d(y, x)$

  • By the linearity of the integral operator, for each $z \in C[a, b]$, we have

    $\begin{array}{rcl} d(x, y) & = & \int_a^b |x(t) - y(t)| \, dt \\ & = & \int_a^b |x(t) - z(t) + z(t) - y(t)| \, dt \\ & \leq & \int_a^b |x(t) - z(t)| + |z(t) - y(t)| \, dt \\ & = & \int_a^b |x(t) - z(t)| \, dt + \int_a^b |z(t) - y(t)| \, dt \\ & = & d(x, z) + d(z, y) \end{array}$
Since all three conditions are satisfied, $d$ is a metric on $C[a, b]$.
