The metric on the sequence space $l^\infty$ is $d(x, y) = \mathrm{sup}_{i \in \mathbb{N}} |x_i - y_i|$, where $x = (x_1, x_2, \ldots)$ and $y = (y_1, y_2, \ldots)$. Let $z = (z_1, z_2, \ldots) \in l^\infty$. For each $i \in \mathbb{N}$, we have

$|x_i - y_i| = |x_i - z_i + z_i - y_i| \leq |x_i - z_i| + |z_i - y_i|$

Taking the supremum on both sides, we have

$\begin{array}{rcl} d(x, y) & = & \mathrm{sup}_{i \in \mathbb{N}} |x_i - y_i| \\ & \leq & \mathrm{sup}_{i \in \mathbb{N}} \left( |x_i - z_i| + |z_i - y_i| \right) \\ & \leq & \mathrm{sup}_{i \in \mathbb{N}} |x_i - z_i| + \mathrm{sup}_{i \in \mathbb{N}} |z_i - y_i| \\ & = & d(x, z) + d(z, y) \end{array}$

Thus, the metric on $l^\infty$ satisfies the triangle inequality.
