The triangle inequality states that $d(x, y) \leq d(x, z) + d(z, y)$, for all $x, y, z \in X$. By rearranging the order of $x$, $y$, and $z$, we have $d(x, z) \leq d(x, y) + d(y, z)$, which implies that

$d(x, z) - d(y, z) \leq d(x, y)$

A different arrangement yields $d(y, z) \leq d(y, x) + d(x, z)$, which implies

$-(d(x, z) - d(y, z)) \leq d(x, y)$

From these two equations, we conclude that

$|d(x, z) - d(y, z)| \leq d(x, y)$
